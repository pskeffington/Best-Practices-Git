\section{Query Framing}

The publication workflow begins with a research query rather than a model. The query should identify the population, outcome, predictors, decision context, and inferential or predictive target. Before modeling begins, the researcher should be able to explain why machine learning is appropriate and what a conventional baseline would miss.

A strong query also defines the intended contribution. The contribution may be methodological, empirical, translational, or evaluative, but it should be stated before model selection to reduce post hoc rationalization.
