# Target Journal Strategy

## Recommended primary target

### Research Integrity and Peer Review

**Publisher:** Springer Nature / BMC  
**Recommended article framing:** Framework / research reporting / peer-review innovation article  
**Fit:** Highest

This is the best initial target because the manuscript directly addresses research reporting, peer review, editorial decision support, publication ethics, and improvement of the usability of research reports.

Recommended submission angle:

> This manuscript proposes a reviewer-readable claim-to-evidence audit trail for STEM manuscripts as a pre-review validation framework for authors, editors, reviewers, and publishers. It is intended to improve the completeness, usability, and reviewability of research reports without replacing expert peer review.

Why it fits:

- The journal explicitly covers research integrity in publication.
- It considers work on peer review and editorial decision-making.
- It considers work on research reporting.
- It particularly encourages work on interventions that improve completeness, accuracy, and usability of research reports.
- It welcomes constructive discussion of novel approaches for increasing the availability, value, and reliability of published research.

## Secondary targets

### Accountability in Research

**Recommended article framing:** Research integrity / accountability / conceptual framework article  
**Fit:** Strong

This journal is a good second target if the manuscript is framed around integrity, accountability, and validity of research results rather than publisher workflow.

Recommended adjustment:

- Emphasize ethical accountability in scientific claims.
- Highlight claim overreach, missing evidence, and reproducibility gaps as research integrity problems.
- Add a stronger discussion of how claim audit trails reduce avoidable misconduct-adjacent risks such as unsupported causal claims, selective reporting, and misleading generalization.

### Learned Publishing

**Recommended article framing:** Scholarly publishing workflow / publisher operations / editorial innovation article  
**Fit:** Strong if practical publisher workflow is emphasized

This is a good target if the manuscript is positioned for publishers, editors, scholarly communications professionals, and publishing operations teams.

Recommended adjustment:

- Emphasize editor triage, publisher intake, machine-readable schemas, and implementation in journal workflows.
- Reduce some STEM-methods detail and increase practical publishing process detail.

### Journal of Scholarly Publishing

**Recommended article framing:** Scholarly publishing technology / editorial workflow article  
**Fit:** Moderate to strong

This journal is appropriate if the manuscript is framed as a publishing-industry tool for editors, authors, and publishers rather than a research integrity framework.

Recommended adjustment:

- Emphasize scholarly publishing infrastructure, technical change, author submission quality, and editor-reviewer burden.

## Conditional targets after empirical validation

### Quantitative Science Studies

**Fit:** Conditional

This would become more attractive after adding an empirical evaluation across a sample of STEM manuscripts. The current framework article is probably too implementation/proposal oriented unless supported by systematic corpus-level evidence.

Recommended upgrade before targeting:

- Run the pre-check tool on 25 to 100 STEM manuscripts.
- Quantify claim counts, common red flags, time saved, and agreement between automated pre-check and human reviewer assessment.

### Research Evaluation

**Fit:** Conditional

Potential fit if the article is expanded into an evaluation of research-quality assessment or editorial decision-making.

Recommended upgrade before targeting:

- Add empirical validation, inter-rater agreement, and publisher workflow evaluation.

## Recommended first submission strategy

Submit first to **Research Integrity and Peer Review**.

Rationale:

1. The manuscript is already framed around peer review, reporting, editorial triage, research integrity, and research-report usability.
2. The journal's scope is closest to the current manuscript without requiring a large empirical study first.
3. The article can be submitted as a framework or constructive discussion with implementation artifacts.
4. The NLSY79 micro-environment provides a worked demonstration, which supports the framework without overclaiming empirical validation.

## Pre-submission edits for primary target

Before submission, make these edits:

1. Add a paragraph explicitly naming the article as a reporting and peer-review intervention.
2. Add a table mapping the framework to the journal's research reporting and peer-review scope.
3. Emphasize that the pre-check does not replace peer review.
4. Add a limitations paragraph noting that empirical validation across multiple manuscripts is future work.
5. Keep the NLSY79 micro-environment as a demonstration rather than proof of effectiveness.

## Final target recommendation

**Primary target:** Research Integrity and Peer Review  
**Backup target:** Accountability in Research  
**Publisher-workflow target:** Learned Publishing  
**Industry/scholarly publishing target:** Journal of Scholarly Publishing  
**Empirical metascience target after validation:** Quantitative Science Studies
