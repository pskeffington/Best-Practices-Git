# Submission Cover Letter

Dear Editor,

Please consider the manuscript, **"Claim Auditability in STEM Manuscripts: A Claim-to-Evidence Audit Trail for Pre-Review Validation,"** for publication as a framework or methods article.

This manuscript addresses a practical problem in scientific publishing: editors and reviewers are often asked to reconstruct the relationship between major manuscript claims, evidence artifacts, methods, validation, limitations, and reproducibility materials from scattered text and supplementary files. Existing reporting guidelines, transparency policies, and reproducibility expectations are valuable, but they do not always provide a claim-level map that makes each major claim auditable before peer review.

The manuscript proposes claim auditability as a pre-review quality attribute for STEM manuscripts. Each major claim is classified, linked to evidence and method support, assigned boundary language, connected to reproducibility or source material where relevant, and given an editorial status before peer review. The goal is not to replace expert review or automate acceptance decisions. The goal is to provide a pre-review validation artifact that helps authors prepare stronger submissions, editors triage incomplete packages, and reviewers evaluate claims more efficiently.

The submission package includes the manuscript source, references, a machine-readable claim-audit schema, a publisher pre-check schema, an author checklist, a reviewer form, and a worked NLSY79 manuscript micro-environment demonstrating the framework in practice. The package is organized to pass its own STEM pre-check logic and is intended to be transparent, reproducible, and editor-readable.

We believe the article will be of interest to readers concerned with scholarly publishing, research integrity, reproducibility, peer-review burden, and STEM manuscript quality control.
