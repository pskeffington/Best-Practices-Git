# Author Contribution and Novelty Statement

## Contribution statement

This manuscript contributes a claim-to-evidence audit trail framework for STEM manuscripts. The framework is designed as a pre-review validation layer that helps authors, editors, reviewers, and publishers determine whether major scientific claims are traceable to appropriate evidence, methods, validation or justification, limitation language, and reproducibility materials.

## Novelty

The manuscript is not simply another reporting checklist. Existing reporting guidelines and transparency policies typically define what authors should disclose. This framework focuses on how those disclosures are organized around the claims that reviewers must evaluate.

The novel contribution is the claim-level audit unit:

```text
Claim
  -> claim level
  -> manuscript location
  -> evidence artifact
  -> method support
  -> validation or justification
  -> boundary language
  -> reproducibility support
  -> reviewer finding
  -> editorial status
```

## Practical value

The framework gives authors a pre-submission check, editors a triage artifact, reviewers a navigable evidence map, and publishers a machine-readable basis for pre-review validation.

## Distinction from peer review

The framework does not determine whether a claim is true and does not replace expert review. It evaluates whether the submitted package contains an inspectable evidence chain adequate for review.

## One-sentence contribution

This manuscript proposes a reviewer-readable claim-to-evidence audit trail for STEM manuscripts that links major claims to evidence, methods, validation, limitations, and reproducibility materials before peer review.
