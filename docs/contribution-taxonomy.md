# Contribution Taxonomy

## Purpose

This document defines the contribution taxonomy for the manuscript and repository. The goal is to make the project's scholarly contribution precise enough for peer review.

## Central contribution

The project contributes a workflow scaffold for machine-learning science that links research questions, study design, data documentation, modeling, validation, ethical review, reproducibility, manuscript writing, and submission preparation into a single auditable process.

## Contribution types

### 1. Scientific-process contribution

The scaffold re-centers machine-learning research on the scientific sequence:

```text
Question -> Design -> Evidence -> Claim -> Publication
```

This contribution matters because many ML projects begin with model selection rather than query discipline or protocol design.

### 2. Reproducibility contribution

The scaffold treats reproducibility as a set of required artifacts rather than an optional appendix. Each stage produces a reviewable object that can be versioned, tested, and linked to the manuscript.

### 3. Claim-discipline contribution

The scaffold introduces evidence-to-claim control. Manuscript claims are assigned levels and linked to supporting artifacts. This reduces overclaiming and makes limitations easier to state precisely.

### 4. Reporting-operationalization contribution

The scaffold operationalizes reporting standards by translating them into upstream workflow steps. Instead of asking only what the final manuscript should disclose, the scaffold asks how those disclosures are produced.

### 5. Manuscript-engineering contribution

The scaffold treats the manuscript as an engineered research output. Manuscript sections are built from prior research artifacts rather than written from memory after analysis is complete.

### 6. Pedagogical contribution

The scaffold can teach students and applied researchers how to move from a research question to a defensible ML manuscript. It exposes the hidden structure of responsible ML research.

### 7. Review-support contribution

The scaffold gives collaborators, supervisors, reviewers, and editors a clearer trail from research design to manuscript claims. It can reduce ambiguity during internal review and peer review.

## Contribution boundary

The project does not propose a new ML estimator, a new validation metric, a replacement for reporting guidelines, or a universal governance framework. Its contribution is workflow-level: it makes existing scientific and reporting expectations executable, modular, versioned, and manuscript-aware.

## Reviewer-facing novelty statement

Existing standards describe what ML manuscripts should report. This project contributes an operational scaffold for producing those reportable elements from the beginning of the scientific process. The scaffold links query framing, protocol design, data auditing, feature planning, model validation, ethics review, reproducibility packaging, and manuscript drafting through version-controlled artifacts and testable workflow objects.

## Best concise contribution statement

This project operationalizes reproducible machine-learning science as a query-to-publication workflow in which every major manuscript claim is traceable to a staged artifact and bounded by the strength of its supporting evidence.
